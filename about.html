About Holy Family

More than four decades of traditional Catholic worship in the Lowcountry.

Holy Family is a traditional Roman Catholic chapel community rooted in reverent liturgy, sacramental life, and the faithful preservation of the traditional Roman Rite.

Our History

A chapel with continuity, gratitude, and a permanent home.

Holy Family Chapel was founded in 1977 in the City of Charleston, South Carolina. The first Mass was offered in November of that year by the late Rev. Fr. Hector L. Bolduc.

In September 2007, with the desire to express more clearly its identity as a pre-Vatican II traditional Roman Catholic community, the members unanimously adopted the name Holy Family Traditional Catholic Chapel.

Through the generosity of Mr. Robert Zehnder, one of the chapel’s early founders and benefactors, Holy Family received the residential property in Summerville that now serves as its permanent chapel and rectory. This gift provided not only a stable home for the chapel, but also room for future growth and expansion.

Mr. Zehnder was called to his eternal reward on January 22, 2007. His generosity and lasting support continue to be remembered with sincere gratitude by the faithful of Holy Family.

Our Life & Mission

A stable traditional Catholic presence in South Carolina.

What Holy Family Provides

  • The reverent celebration of the Traditional Latin Mass
  • A stable place for the reception of the sacraments
  • A prayerful chapel life centered on devotion, reverence, and continuity
  • A home for individuals and families seeking the traditional Roman Rite

Over the Years

  • Continual presence in the Lowcountry for more than forty years
  • Growth extending across South Carolina, Georgia, and North Carolina
  • Conversions to the Catholic faith
  • The return of many “lost” or homesick Catholics to their roots

Holy Family has maintained a stable and enduring presence as the oldest independent Roman Catholic congregation in South Carolina, offering the faithful a permanent place for traditional Catholic worship and sacramental life.

Our Worship

The traditional Roman Rite at the center of chapel life.

Holy Family worships exclusively according to the traditional Roman Rite, in continuity with the form codified by the Council of Trent and Pope St. Pius V.

The chapel exists to provide a stable place where the perennial Catholic faith, the Traditional Latin Mass, and the sacramental life may be lived and handed on with reverence and fidelity.

We are committed to the faith that formed our ancestors, the worship that nourished the saints, and the traditions that expressed the Church’s life for centuries.

Looking Ahead

Faithfully preserving what has been received.

The members of Holy Family remain dedicated to preserving their Roman Catholic faith and heritage through prayer, sacrifice, stability, and devotion to the sacred liturgy.

As a self-governing chapel community, Holy Family continues its mission of providing a lasting home for traditional Catholic worship in the Lowcountry, with gratitude for the past and confidence in God’s providence for the future.
