Our Clergy

Priests dedicated to the sacred liturgy and the care of souls.

Holy Family is served by traditional Catholic priests committed to reverent worship, sound doctrine, sacramental life, and the faithful pastoral care of the chapel community.

A Brief History

Continuity of priestly service at Holy Family.

In the early years of Holy Family’s existence, the chapel was served for a time by priests of the late Archbishop Marcel Lefebvre’s Society of St. Pius X (SSPX) and by priests affiliated with the Society.

When the Society was no longer able to serve the faithful in South Carolina, the members of Holy Family sought validly ordained traditional priests who were willing to travel regularly in order to continue offering the Holy Sacrifice of the Mass and the sacraments until a full-time priest could be found.

Since late February 2008, Holy Family has been served by the Franciscan Friars of the Convent of St. John the Baptist, and the chapel has remained an important center of their apostolate for many years.

Their Ministry

What the clergy provide for the faithful.

Liturgical & Sacramental Life

  • Celebration of the Traditional Latin Mass
  • Hearing Confessions and providing spiritual counsel
  • Administration of the sacraments and sacramentals
  • Reverent observance of the traditional Roman Rite

Pastoral Care

  • Preaching and catechesis for the faithful
  • Guidance for individuals and families seeking a deeper devotional life
  • Support for those returning to traditional Catholic worship
  • Steady priestly presence in the life of the chapel community

Due Diligence & Fidelity

Careful stewardship in the service of souls.

In keeping with the chapel’s By-Laws and Constitutions, Holy Family conducted due diligence before entrusting the care of souls to the Friars. This included the review of their religious and sacramental credentials, as well as criminal background checks, all of which were satisfactorily completed.

The priests serving Holy Family make the Profession of Faith and the Oath Against Modernism, renewing these commitments annually in continuity with the discipline expressed in the 1917 Code of Canon Law.

The clergy attached to Holy Family are dedicated to the reverent celebration of the sacred liturgy, the faithful transmission of Catholic doctrine, and the spiritual care of the faithful.

Contacting a Priest

Questions about the sacraments, catechism, or spiritual direction.

For specific questions about the sacraments, catechism, spiritual guidance, or pastoral concerns, please contact the rectory and request to speak with a priest.

The clergy of Holy Family strive to serve the faithful with prayer, reverence, and steadiness, so that the life of the chapel remains centered on Almighty God and the sanctification of souls.
